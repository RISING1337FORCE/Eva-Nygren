About Vasaborgen

Vasaborgen is a historical building located at Stockholmsvägen 60, in the Hersby area of Lidingö. Originally constructed in 1923, this building has undergone several transformations over the years, adapting to the changing needs of the community while retaining its historical charm. The building is known for its distinct architectural features, including a classicist facade and marked corners.

Vasaborgen has served multiple purposes since its construction. Initially built as a private residence, it later accommodated a variety of businesses, including a cinema, post office, and police facilities. Over the years, it has remained a significant landmark in Lidingö, recognized for its contribution to the area's cultural and architectural heritage.

The Story of Vasaborgen

Vasaborgen is a historic building located on Lidingö, Sweden. Constructed in 1923, it was designed by architect Anton Wallby in a classicist style, with a plastered facade and distinct corners. Initially, it was a private residence, and over time, the building has had several different uses.

For many years, Vasaborgen served as a home for multiple families. In the 1920s, it housed a cinema in the basement, which operated until 1962. The building also accommodated local businesses, including a post office, a laundry and ironing service, police facilities with an arrest, and even a gingerbread bakery that filled the area with delightful aromas.

The park adjacent to Vasaborgen, Hästskojarparken, was created around the same time as the building, featuring a sculpture named "Hästskojaren" by Lidingö artist Sven Lundqvist, installed in 2009.

Today, Vasaborgen is a significant landmark on Lidingö, reflecting the island’s architectural heritage and its evolution over the past century. Despite the changes over the years, it continues to play an integral role in the local community, blending its historical charm with the modern developments of the area.
